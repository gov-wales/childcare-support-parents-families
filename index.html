Childcare support for parents and families

Guidance for parents and carers on the main funded childcare and family support schemes, who can access them, and how to apply.

Overview

There is a range of support available to help parents living in Wales with the cost of childcare.

Eligibility for support will depend on factors such as:

  • the age of your child
  • your circumstances
  • whether you are employed

If you are not living in Wales, you can find out about similar schemes in:

  • Scotland
  • England
  • Northern Ireland

Support for parents of children aged 9 months up to 2 years

Tax-free childcare

If you are a working parent or carer, you could get a £2 top-up for every £8 that you pay towards childcare

You can use it to pay for childcare including:

  • childminders, nurseries and nannies
  • before and after school clubs
  • holiday clubs
  • specialist equipment for the childcare provider to use for your child, such as mobility aids

Adopted children are eligible, but foster children are not.

If your child is disabled and usually lives with you, you may get up to £4,000 a year until 1 September after their 16th birthday.

Find out more about tax-free childcare on childcarechoices.gov.uk.

Universal Credit childcare

If you are a working family and in receipt of Universal Credit you could be eligible to receive up to 85% support for your childcare costs.

You could claim for all the children under 17 you are responsible for, until the 31 August after their 16th birthday.

You will have to pay for your childcare costs before any reimbursement unless you are eligible for an initial up-front payment. Learn about up-front childcare costs on childcarechoices.gov.uk.

You cannot apply if you are in receipt of any of the following:

  • Tax Credits
  • Tax-Free Childcare

Learn more about Universal Credit childcare on gov.uk.

Help for students with children

Student Finance Wales childcare grant

If you are an undergraduate student, you could get help with childcare costs from Student Finance Wales.

You could get 85% of your childcare costs, up to a maximum of:

  • £192 per week for 1 dependent child
  • £329 per week for 2 or more dependent children

To be eligible for the childcare grant, you must:

  • have at least one child under 15 (under 17 if they have special educational needs)
  • use a childcare provider registered with Care Inspectorate Wales
  • be enrolled on a full-time or part-time course (including a distance learning)
  • be getting undergraduate student finance

If you don't have a childcare provider at the time you apply, you can still get this grant, but the amount will vary.

You cannot apply if you:

  • claim the childcare element of Universal Credit or Working Tax Credit
  • get tax-free childcare from HM Revenue and Customs
  • are paying a relative to care for your child only
  • or your partner are receiving funding from the NHS

Learn more about the childcare grant at studentfinancewales.co.uk.

NHS childcare allowance

If you are a medical or dental students who receives the NHS Bursary, you can apply for help towards the cost of registered childcare whilst attending your course.

Find out more about the NHS Childcare Allowance on nhs.uk.

Support for parents of children aged 2 years

12.5 hours of weekly childcare

You could be eligible for 12.5 hours per week of high quality, part-time childcare.

Welsh Government's Flying Start programme helps families with children under 4 years in disadvantaged areas.

In the most deprived areas, support can also include:

  • enhanced health visits
  • access to parenting support schemes
  • help with speech, language and communication development

Flying Start childcare is currently only available in certain areas. We are working with local authorities to increase the availability of childcare for 2 year olds in more areas of Wales.

Find out more about the Flying Start programme.

Contact your local authority to find out if you live in a Flying Start area.

Support for parents of children aged 3 and 4 years

Nursery education provision

Part time nursery education is available for all three and four year olds, depending on when their birthday falls.

Child's birthday Funded nursery places start
1 December to 31 December First day of term on or after 1 January
1 January to 31 March First day of term on or after 1 April
1 April to 31 August First day of term on or after 1 September

All local authorities must offer a minimum of 10 hours per week for 39 weeks (the school year).

You do not need to be in work to get a nursery education place for your child.

Getting your nursery education hours

The exact allocation of nursery education hours is dependent on the local authority area where you live. You should not receive more than 30 hours per week of combined nursery education and childcare hours.

Contact your local authority to apply for a funded nursery place.

20 hours of weekly funded childcare for eligible working families

If you are a working parent or enrolled in certain education and training courses, you could claim up to 30 hours of nursery education and childcare a week, for up to 48 weeks of the year.

During term-time, the 30 hours per week comprises:

  • 10 hours of nursery education minimum (increased to 30 during school holidays)
  • 20 hours of childcare maximum

The 48 weeks per year offer includes 9 weeks of school holiday provision at 30 hours a week.

Eligibility criteria

To be eligible for this provision you must:

  • live in Wales
  • have a child aged 3 or 4 years old
  • have a gross income of £100,000 or less per tax year

You must also be one of the following:

  • employed and earn at least (on average) the equivalent to 16 hours a week at National Minimum Wage or Living Wage
  • enrolled on a Higher Education undergraduate or post graduate course at a publicly funded institution that is at least 10 weeks in length (including courses delivered via distance learning)
  • enrolled on a Further Education course at a publicly funded institution or school-based sixth form college that is at least 10 weeks in length (not including distance learning courses)
  • be on Statutory Pay and Leave (Sick, Maternity, Paternity, Parental, Bereavement or Adoption Leave)

Check if you are eligible for the Childcare Offer for Wales.

Getting your nursery education hours

The exact allocation of nursery education hours is dependent on the local authority area where you live. You should not receive more than 30 hours per week of combined nursery education and childcare hours.

Contact your local authority to apply for a funded nursery place.

Help for children with additional support needs

If you are eligible to access the Childcare offer for Wales and you believe your child has an additional support need, you can get further help from the Childcare Offer for Wales Additional Support Grant.

This grant is not a financial payment made directly to you. Your local authority can draw on this grant fund to help ensure that a child who requires additional support is able to access the childcare element of the Childcare Offer for Wales in the same way as other eligible children.

Contact the Childcare Offer for Wales helpline to find out more about the additional support grant.

Choosing a childcare provider

There are hundreds of registered nurseries, child minders, Cylch Meithrin, playgroups and crèches to choose from.

Learn more about the different types of childcare available to you on childcareinformation.wales.

You can access English, Welsh or bilingual childcare, depending on your preference. Pre-school is a great time to also start your child's bilingual journey.

Learn about the benefits of your child learning Welsh on meithrin.cymru.

Contact your local authority for help finding a nursery education or childcare provider that meets your needs.
